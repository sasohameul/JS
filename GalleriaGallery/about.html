GALLERIAGALLERY

세계적인 권위의 전시회, GALLERIAGALLERY

GALLERIAGALLERY는 ‘미술관의 미술관’이란 뜻으로, 1892년 미국 뉴욕에서 당시 상류사회 여자들을 위한 발행물로 시작 되었습니다. 130년의 전통을 지닌 GALLERIAGALLERY는 ‘하이패션의 바이블’, ‘세계에서 가장 유명한 전시 잡지’ 등 패션 미디어로서의 권위와 대표성을 견고하게 쌓아왔습니다. GALLERIAGALLERY는 다양한 플랫폼을 넘나들며 전 세계 27개 마켓에서 발행되고 있으며 2억 7천 만명 이상의 오디언스를 지닌 세계적 권위의 전시회모음집입니다.

1996년에 창간된 GALLERIAGALLERY는 글로벌 전시회의 위상과 궤를 같이하여, 가장 영향력 있는 패션 매거진으로서 독보적인 리더십을 이어가고 있습니다. 프린트 매거진 뿐아니라 웹, 모바일, 소셜 미디어 등 디지털 플랫폼에서도 시대의 흐름에 맞는 콘텐츠 기획과 접근으로 새로운 세대를 지속적으로 사로잡으며 강력한 하나의 브랜드로 존재감을 발휘하고 있습니다.

The most remarkable feature of Maria Svarbova's artwork is her ability to create astonishing harmony through the appropriate combination of old and new elements. Svarbova delicately balances between the new and the old, allowing viewers to fully immerse themselves in her works. This interaction between the new and the old has served as a valuable reference for constructing the exhibition titled "FUTURO RETRO,"
which explores the concept of "yesterday's future."

Maria's unique style focuses on experimentation with space, color, and atmosphere, breaking away from traditional portraiture. With an interest in architecture and public spaces from the era of socialism, Maria transforms each scene with her creative palette, emphasizing the range and depth of her modern freshness. The meticulous composition of figures and ordinary objects creates thematic and dreamlike scenes. Her images maintain a quiet tension that hints at new possibilities within the clean and smooth rhythm of the surface. Maria's postmodern vision boldly expresses a dialogue that compels viewers to respond to the mysteries, loneliness, and isolation of the human experience. Nevertheless, her style, deeply rooted in subdued pastel tones, offers a way for the viewer's gaze to continually transform the humble beauty of life into a state of perpetual reverence.

Alex Stoddard (born November 15, 1993) is an American fine art photographer currently residing in Orange, CA. Visual artist and photographer Alex Stoddard focuses on the human form and the process of infusing it with natural surroundings. He also strives to create whimsical and surreal portraits. In our interview with Alex, we talk about doing a 365 project when he was only 16 and how it impacted his life and art.

Alex spent much of his early life alone exploring the woods behind his family home. He considers this isolation as well as the influence of social media to be major factors in his decision to start taking selfportraits at the age of 16. He was inspired by the way he could use photography to escape his mundane life by putting on costumes and posing as different characters in his made-up worlds. The blank canvas of the human body and its connectedness to nature became a major factor in his images.

2005

Art has always been, first and foremost, a means of escape for me and those who view my work. It is a distraction from reality, a literal building of a new world to live in, even if just for a moment as someone scrolls by the picture on their social media feed. The real world is grim, lacking in magic. My photography is meant to transport, in the same way that a movie or song invites the audience into a different little pocket of reality.

A dark surrealist aesthetic mixed with ideas taken from art history, the human form, or nature, is Stoddard's signature. With these simple compositions that frame meaningful themes, the artist manages to use self-portraiture as a means to escape, drawing the viewer in with him to a dream-state: “My photography is meant to transport, in the same way that a movie or song invites the audience into a different little pocket of reality.”

In Yoshihiko Ueda's photographs, the subjects are mostly things we have actually seen. When capturing natural elements, he delicately portrays the surface, allowing the flow of light to be distinctly revealed. Even if the results do not turn out as expected, Yoshihiko advises not to be discouraged and to trust our instincts, continuing to follow the desired path. Through him, the message is conveyed that it is more important to consistently and steadily pursue the desired path rather than focusing solely on the end result.
